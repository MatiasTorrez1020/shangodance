OUR DANCES

1. Ritmos Latinos

Salsa

Salsa is a vibrant and energetic dance form originating from Latin America, known for its quick steps, intricate turns, and playful partner work. It's a social dance that emphasizes rhythm and connection.

Characteristics:

  • Fast-paced, high energy
  • Lead and follow partner dance
  • Features spins, shimmies, and body isolations
  • Strong emphasis on hip movement

Mambo

Mambo is an energetic and expressive dance characterized by its distinctive "break" step, dynamic turns, and strong Afro-Cuban influences. It's often performed with flair and passion.

Characteristics:

  • Originated from Cuba, influenced by swing and jazz
  • Emphasis on the "Mambo break" (forward/backward step)
  • Features open breaks and strong hip action
  • Often performed on 2 (count)

Cha Cha Cha

The Cha-cha-chá is a lively, rhythmic dance of Cuban origin, named for the sound of the dancers' shoes on the floor. It's famous for its distinctive "cha-cha-cha" triple step.

Characteristics:

  • Triple step (cha-cha-cha)
  • Playful and flirtatious movements
  • Syncopated rhythm
  • Often danced to Latin pop or authentic Cuban music

Bachata

Bachata is a social dance from the Dominican Republic, characterized by its sensual movements, close partner connection, and signature "tap" or "pop" on the fourth beat.

Characteristics:

  • Romantic and sensual style
  • Prominent hip movement (especially for ladies)
  • Includes a "tap" or "pop" on the 4th/8th beat
  • Can be danced in close or open position

Kizomba

Kizomba is a beautiful and intimate partner dance originating from Angola, known for its smooth, slow, and sensual movements, and a strong connection between partners.

Characteristics:

  • Close embrace, intimate connection
  • Slow, sensual, and flowing movements
  • Strong lead and follow communication
  • Often includes body waves and dips

2. Ritmos de Salón

Sport Dance

Sport Dance (often referring to Ballroom and Latin American Dancesport) combines technical precision, athleticism, and artistry. It's highly competitive and visually stunning.

Characteristics:

  • Highly structured and technical
  • Focus on posture, frame, and footwork
  • Competitive aspect with specific rules
  • Includes both Standard (Waltz, Tango, Foxtrot) and Latin (Cha-cha, Samba, Rumba) styles

Tangos

Argentine Tango is a passionate and improvisational partner dance with deep roots in Buenos Aires. It is characterized by its dramatic pauses, close embrace, and intricate leg movements.

Characteristics:

  • Close embrace, strong connection
  • Improvisational, relies on lead and follow
  • Dramatic pauses and sharp movements
  • Features "ochos," "gira," and "sacadas"

Valses

Waltz is a classic ballroom dance known for its elegant, flowing, and rotational movements. The Viennese Waltz is faster and more exhilarating, while the Slow Waltz is more graceful and controlled.

Characteristics:

  • Smooth, continuous turns and rotations
  • Elegant and graceful posture
  • Strong sense of musicality to 3/4 time signature
  • Both Slow (English) and Viennese variations

Boleros

Bolero is a slow, romantic Latin dance, often called the "dance of love." It combines elements of Rumba, Waltz, and Tango, creating a sensuous and dramatic experience.

Characteristics:

  • Slow, expressive, and romantic
  • Combines Rumba, Tango, and Waltz elements
  • Known for dramatic pauses and sustained movements
  • Strong emotional connection between partners

3. Ritmos Folklóricos

Folklore Nacional

Our National Folklore dances delve into the rich cultural heritage of [Your Country/Region, e.g., Bolivia], showcasing traditional steps, costumes, and music that tell stories of our land and people.

Characteristics:

  • Authentic traditional dances of [Your Country/Region]
  • Celebrates local customs and history
  • Often performed in traditional attire
  • Varies greatly by region (e.g., Caporales, Morenada, Cueca)

Folklore Internacional

Explore the diverse world of international folklore through dances from various cultures. Learn about the global tapestry of traditional movements, music, and storytelling.

Characteristics:

  • Dances from different countries and cultures
  • Exploration of global dance traditions
  • Understanding diverse rhythms and expressions
  • Examples: Samba (Brazil), Flamenco (Spain), Irish Step Dance

Primer Nivel: Foundational Training

Prior to advancing into specific dance rhythms, all aspiring dancers at Shango Dance Company must successfully complete our comprehensive **Primer Nivel**. This foundational program ensures every student develops a strong understanding of essential dance principles and prepares them for specialized training.

  • **Terminología de Ballets:** Understanding the fundamental vocabulary and positions of classical ballet, crucial for all dance forms.
  • **Terminología de Ritmos de Salón:** Familiarization with the specific terms, figures, and concepts used in ballroom and social dance.
  • **Terminología de Folklore:** Learning the specialized terminology associated with national and international folkloric dance styles.
  • **Teorías y conceptos de escenarios, shows y performances:** Gaining theoretical knowledge about stage presence, showmanship, performance dynamics, and audience engagement.
  • **Expresión corporal, facial y manejo de escenario:** Developing control over body and facial expressions, and mastering effective stage movement and blocking.
  • **Timing y musicalidad:** Cultivating a deep connection to music, understanding rhythm, tempo, and how to interpret musicality through movement.
